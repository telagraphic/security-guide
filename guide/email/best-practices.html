Best Practices

  • Have more than one email address, perhaps one for newsletters and leisurely stuff

  • Use different passwords for each email account, and use a strong one. Use a password generator that comes with a password manager!

  • Do not click on random emails and the links or document attachments in them. That is how you get hacked. It is called phishing

  • Be careful about clicking links that look to be an 'official website', hackers camouflage their malicious attacks

  • It is a good idea to not send passwords, bank details, confidential information over an email. Just not smart especially if you are someone important in a business or politician

  • Try not to connect third party apps to your account, what if that 3rd party gets hacked? They potentially have a way into your account now.

  • Don't download images, set your email settings to simple or text only

  • Switch to a secure email service! Google reads your emails and everything you wrote in them!

  • If you are using company email on public wi-fi, then use a VPN or just don't do it

  • Delete old email, make it a quarterly or at least annual habit. Less is more!
