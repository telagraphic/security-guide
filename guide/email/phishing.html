Email Phishing

Look but don't click! Clicking on links from random emails can take you to a malicious site Downloading attachments from random emails can download malicous code, aka you are now at risk of being hacked Hover over the email sender and see if it matches what website they say they are from Misleading domain names that look like apple.fakewebsite.com are meant to fool you. If the domain name looks suspicious, it probably is Spelling mistakes in the email is a dead give away Vague greetings are common phishing openers, most brands will use your name! Urgent or threatening language is a common tactic to scare you, don't be scared Lack of contact information at the end of the email, most legit businesses want their contact information visible Appears to be from the government. Most government agencies require official paperwork. Call the agency to verify the matter at hand. Asking for money is a dead give away
