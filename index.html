Clear the smoke on digital security with curated content, how to guides, tools and current trends to protect your online life

Knowledge is Power

Online security can be complicated, boring and unfamiliar territory for the average person. It's not worth the effort for most people.

This guide aims to clarify and provide practical steps to protect your digital life. There are basic steps you can take that can increase your security and privacy.

Privacy And Security

Privacy is concerned about how much personal information you share and with who. Protecting your data privacy is the goal of security. Being confident in the security measures you take to protect your data is equally important.

Snowden Revelations

It's true, the NSA and many other intelligence agencies have access to virtually anything we do electronically. They own the DNA of the internet. Mass surviellance is here to stay. They can hack, track, and identify you if they want to devote the people, money and time. But the government isn't the only threat.

Data = Money

Corporations, apps, and websites want your information. Criminals, hackers, and bad people want your information. The things they can do with our information is endless.

Simple steps can block prying hands. This guide is here to help.

    Internet Overview

    Refresh your mental model of the Internet.

    Internet Service Provider

    Learn how your ISP tracks and sells your data and what you can do about it.

    Web Browser

    Learn about new privacy browsers, blocks ads with extensions, and understand how private browsing works.

    Domain Name System

    DNS is the phone book of the Internet. Easily upgrade to a safer and faster service, block bad sites with a simple text file and prevent data leaks that reveal your search and IP address.

    Search Engines

    Use an alternate search engine that doesn't track you. Most of them use Google under the hood but strip out data that can be used to identify you.

    Virtual Private Network & TOR

    Understand how a VPN can increase your privacy and pick a proven provider. Use TOR for anonymous browsing and why you might need it.

    Router

    The most important part of this guide! Level up your router's security with a checklist from a computer expert. Understand the hazards of Wi-Fi and tools to protect you.

    Passwords

    Passwords are out, passphrases are in. Learn about the new official password recommendations and start using a password manager to make life easier and safer.

    Email

    It's illegal to open someone else's mail, find a new service that doesn't and learn to spot deceptive phishing scams.

    Mobile Devices

    Set up basic security, 2 Factor Authentication and understand how apps 'share' your data.

    Social Media

    Get off Facebook with a privacy respecting platform and then realize there are 10+ databases that display your information publicly. Don't despair, you can have it hidden or removed.

    Encryption

    If your device gets stolen, encryption can protect your personal data. Encryption is a basic human right, so put it to practice.
