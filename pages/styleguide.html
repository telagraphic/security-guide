Chapter Introduction

The browser is the application that connects to the world wide web from your computer/device. It works with DNS and HTTP to fetch websites and read the HTML, CSS, code and other content for the browser to display.

Risks

  • 3rd-party cookies from advertisers track you across websites and collect your clicks

  • These tracking cookies can 'fingerprint' your identity build a profile of your online habits

  • Visiting non-HTTPS websites leaves your communications unencrypted

  • Private browsing does not protect your information from your employer or ISP!

Fixes

    Check out new browsers that emphasize security and privacy

    Increase your online security and privacy with browser extensions

    HTTPS is becoming a standard, learn how to set it up

    Understand what private browsing does and what you might really need to do

    TOR browser will hide your traffic from your employer or ISP or any network.

    Read more about browsers and privacy
